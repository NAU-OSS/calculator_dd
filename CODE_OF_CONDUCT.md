# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge
In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming community, we pledge to make participation in Calculator DD a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of background or identity.

## Community Values
We value:
- Respectful, constructive collaboration
- Clear communication and inclusive language
- Support for new contributors and maintainers
- Responsible disclosure and safety

## Expected Behavior
- Be friendly, patient, and courteous.
- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Give and accept respectful, constructive feedback.
- Respect privacy and confidentiality when discussing sensitive issues.
- Report problems and help others resolve them when possible.

## Unacceptable Behavior
The following are examples of unacceptable behavior and will not be tolerated:
- Harassment, intimidation, or threats (verbal or written).
- Discriminatory language or slurs based on race, gender, religion, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected characteristics.
- Personal attacks, insults, or sustained disruption of discussions.
- Publishing others' private information (doxing).
- Sexual language or imagery in public spaces.
- Repeatedly ignoring requests to stop unacceptable behavior.

## How to Report a Violation
If you witness or experience a violation, please report it as soon as possible:
- Preferred public route: open an issue at https://github.com/NAU-OSS/calculator_dd/issues and add the label `conduct` (if comfortable).
- Confidential route: email maintainers at NAU-OSS-conduct@proton.me with:
  - A description of the incident (who, what, when, where)
  - Any relevant links or screenshots
  - Preferred outcome or steps you’d like taken

All reports will be handled promptly and with discretion.

## Enforcement and Consequences
- Maintainers will review reports and may consult with others confidentially.
- Responses may include: private warnings, temporary or permanent removal from project communications, blocking, or referral to platform/site administrators.
- Enforcement decisions will consider severity, context, and prior behavior. Appeals may be made by emailing the conduct contact above; maintainers will document decisions and rationale internally.

## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces (code repositories, issue trackers, mailing lists, chat channels, events) and to public behavior reasonably related to project activities.

## Attribution
This Code of Conduct is based on widely used community standards (including the Contributor Covenant and recommendations from opensource.guide) and adapted for Calculator DD.
